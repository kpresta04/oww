Welcome Protect Promote Integrate

Despair is contagious, so is hope. Let's all become infected by hope and spread it to all we meet

Our warm welcome is a locally based charity that looks to welcome asylum seekers and refugees housed in St Helens by the Home Office. We provide support through our weekly drop in the Welcome Café. At the café our friends can access casework support from the British Red Cross, Health advice from the Parish nurse from St Andrews. Attend one of our free English classes. Mots of all they get time to sit and chat with people from St Helens and across the world. In addition to providing a warm welcome we look to protect our friends with the support of Merseyside police who regularly attend the café. Finally, we take every opportunity to bust some of the myths created by the media and help the people of St Helens understand the talents and gifts our friends offer to the local community. Everything we do is to help our friends integrate into the St Helens community so that together we can have a safer, brighter future.

About me

I am Kevin Duffy in 2015 working with a small group of volunteers, St Helens council and members of St Helens Faith forum we began to prepare for arrival of asylum seekers in St Helens. In the spring of 2016 with the help of the YMCA, British Red Cross, SHAP and Refugee women connect we opened the doors of the welcome café for the first time. In the autumn of 2017, we registered Our warm welcome with the charity commission. This was in response to the greater understanding of the needs of our friends. As each month passes, we connect with new groups and individuals willing to help our work. If you feel you have something to offer us, please contact me personally.

Kevin Duffy
